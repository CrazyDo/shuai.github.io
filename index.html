Research vision

My work sits at the interface of human microbiome science, multi-omics integration, host genetics, and computational medicine.

1. Human microbiome as a disease-linked ecosystem

Characterizing how bacterial, fungal, metabolic, and host factors vary across populations and connect to cardiometabolic, cognitive, and immune-related traits.

gut microbiome gut mycobiome population cohorts

2. Multi-omics maps of host-microbe biology

Integrating metagenomics, metabolomics, host genetics, and clinical phenotypes to identify interpretable patterns and biologically grounded hypotheses.

multi-omics metabolomics host genetics

3. Computational medicine with reusable tools

Building analysis workflows and open resources that help translate high-dimensional microbiome data into mechanisms, biomarkers, and therapeutic opportunities.

bioinformatics machine learning open science
